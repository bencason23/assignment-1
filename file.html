Nelson Mandela

1918 - 2013 | Anti-apartheid revolutionary, political leader, and philanthropist

A Life of Courage and Compassion

Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary, political leader, and philanthropist who served as President of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. He was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully representative democratic election.

His government focused on dismantling the legacy of apartheid by tackling institutionalised racism and fostering racial reconciliation. Ideologically an African nationalist and socialist, he served as the president of the African National Congress (ANC) party from 1991 to 1997.

Key Moments in Mandela's Life

1918
Born Rolihlahla Mandela in Mvezo, South Africa
1944
Joined the African National Congress (ANC) and began actively opposing apartheid
1962
Arrested and sentenced to five years in prison
1964
Sentenced to life imprisonment during the Rivonia Trial
1990
Released from prison after 27 years
1993
Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize jointly with F.W. de Klerk
1994
Became South Africa's first black president in the country's first democratic election
1999
Stepped down after one term as president
2013
Passed away at the age of 95

Mandela's Enduring Legacy

Peace and Reconciliation

Mandela's commitment to forgiveness and reconciliation helped South Africa transition from apartheid to democracy without descending into civil war.

Human Rights Advocacy

Even after his presidency, Mandela continued to advocate for human rights, peace, and social justice worldwide.

The Nelson Mandela Foundation

Established in 1999, the foundation promotes Mandela's vision of freedom and equality for all through dialogue and advocacy.
